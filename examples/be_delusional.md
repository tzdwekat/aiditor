**Existence Precedes Essence**  
[Jimmy Kimmel’s Full Interview with Kanye West](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PmZjaYdS3fA)  
“I started producing and selling beats \- and that started going well \- and that wasnt going to work \- and that's started doing well, etc.”  
In his interview with Jimmy Kimmel, Kanye talks about how he was constantly put into a box whenever he tried something new. He consistently proved everyone who doubted him wrong and exceeded everyone's expectations. In fact, this shows Jean Paul Sartre’s philosophy on bad faith in full effect. 

Jean-Paul Sartre was a French philosopher, playwright, and novelist born in 1905\. He's considered the father of existentialism and was one of the most influential thinkers of the 20th century.

According to Jean-Paul Sartre, to behave in "bad faith" is to act as if you are not free to make choices.

* But by sticking with the safe, easy, default “choice,” you become like an object in the world.  
* Only by making difficult choices can you realize your full potential as a human being.


We often put ourselves in boxes with the belief that we are tied down to one certain avenue of life due to one thing or another, whether that be circumstance, society, or physical ability. But Sartre argues for the radical freedom of human beings. For us, existence precedes essence; we are free to create our own meaning and identity through our choices. In other words, our life is what we make it. We only pin ourselves down to one thing due to bad faith \- we can do anything we want if we put our mind to it, and when we come to this realization its can become daunting, especially if we DONT want to be doing what were doing. But we should view this as freeing because we truly are the authors of our own lives, meaning the only thing standing between you and your dreams is whether or not YOU believe it, which is where this confidence that your beliefs will become reality comes from.

**\*Psychology of maintaining positive illusions\***  
So, is there any science to back up how Kanye’s self-confidence helps him achieve his goals? In a paper by Shelley Taylor titled “Positive Illusions.” Shelley finds that mentally healthy and high-functioning individuals consistently see themselves better than they actually are. Meaning that to be successful, your belief in your abilities has to exceed your actual skill level. But what's even more interesting is that she found that people with clinical depression were found to have more accurate assessments of their skill levels. Meaning if you’re too much of a realist about yourself and your abilities and don’t boost yourself up, you’re opening the door to a depressive state, and who wants that? She also found that people who were overconfident in their abilities actually had higher social status than those who didn’t, meaning your confidence in yourself, even if blind, makes those around you like you more. Your audience is a product of your confidence.
